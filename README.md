
# Personal-Spending-Analysis
Analysis of bank data from using Excel to identify trends and provide insights with spending behavior

## File
[Personal Spending Analysis.xlsx](https://github.com/user-attachments/files/27080932/Personal.Spending.Analysis.xlsx)

## Objective
The goal of this project is to understand where money is being spent and identify opportunites to reduce unnecessary expenses

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables
- Charts
- Slicers

## Key Features
- Interactive dashboard
- Spending by Category analysis
- Spending over time
- Summary metrics (toals, average, highest expense)

## Insights
- The highest Spending category is Groceries
- Total Spending decreased by 25.4% from February to March
- Reducing grocery spending by 20% would lower its share of total spending from 47% to approximately 42%.

# Questions and Answers from Dataset
These are the 8 main questions needed that help to analyze the data
1.	What is the total spending across all transactions?
- Total Spending across February and March is $2,199.85
2.	What is the average transaction amount?
- The average transaction amount is 41.51 
3.	Which category has the highest total spending?
- The category with the highest total spending is groceries at 1035.40
4.	How did total spending change from February to March?
- Total spending decreased by 25.2% from February to March, indicating a reduction in overall expenses.
5.	Which category increased the most month-over-month?
- Out of the two categories that increased from February to March, Debt Payment and shopping, Shopping increased the most at from 9.62 to 131.40 (1266% increase). This is due to a very low spending in February
6.	Which category decreased the most?
- The category that decreased the most was Groceries at an overall decrease of 53.2% from February to March. 
7.	Are there any noticeable spending spikes during specific time periods?
- Spending increased for Groceries category on 2/17, 2/23, and 3/16. This corresponds to large grocery delivery days 
8.	How much could I save by reducing a category by 20%?
- Reducing grocery spending by 20% would lower its share of total spending from 47% to approximately 42%.
